Step 1: Steam Your Milk

The first step to make your perfect latte is to grab your milk of choice and get it ready to be steamed.

  • Pick your favorite milk and pour the desired amount into a steam pitcher.
    (Keep in mind the milk will aerate and grow in volume a bit.)
  • Now, purge your steam wand of any water built up inside
  • Place the wand slightly below the surface of the milk at a slight angle and to one side of the pitcher.
  • Turn on the steam wand and barely lower your pitcher to allow the wand to kiss the surface and allow some air in.
  • Submerge the wand below the surface and try to create a whirlpool with the milk.
  • Continue to steam the milk until you reach a temperature of 140-160 °F.
  • Tap the milk pictcher a few times on the counter to get rid of any bubbles that are too large.
    (Your milk should look like glossy paint.)

You can now continue to making the espresso.

Step 2: Prepare Your Shot

Once your milk is finished, it's time to prepare your espresso.

  • Place your portafilter under the grinder.
  • Dose 18-20 grams of espresso into the portafilter basket
  • Tamp the espresso down using approximately 30 lbs of pressure.
  • Take the portafilter and attach it to the grouphead.

Now you are ready to pull your shot!

Step 3: Pull Your Shot

Now you need to pull your shot and make sure it is within parameters and is up to standards.

  • Once your portafilter is secured into the goruphead,
    you can press the double shot button on the machine.
  • Your shot will start to pull, starting as a darker color and slowly becoming a light tan color.
  • The espresso should look like warm honey pouring off a spoon.
    (If it is too fast your grind may have been too course or your tamp may not have been heavy enough.
    If the espresso trickles out very slowly you may have tamped too hard or your grind may have been too fine.)
  • The shot should have pulled between 22-28 seconds in order to be up to standards
  • Pour the shot into a little bit of the milk or syrup in order to save it and prevent a bitter shot.

You are ready for the final step in preparing your latte!

Step 4: Pour Your Milk into the Espresso.

This last step can be as simple or as adnvanced as you would like.

If you want to do it simply, all you have to do is grab your cup and pour the milk straight into the espresso and you are finished!
